01 — Brand Story

Who we are

"We sell electronics, but what we really sell is peace of mind — knowing your stuff will work, and if it doesn't, someone will fix it properly."

Tirath Electronics is a neighbourhood electronics shop — the kind of place where you can buy a new appliance, get an old one repaired, and actually get a straight answer about what's wrong with it.

We serve everyone — students picking up their first headphones, families upgrading the TV, grandparents bringing in a radio that's been through three decades. No talking down, no upselling, no nonsense.

The brand should always feel like that one trusted relative who knows electronics — knowledgeable, calm, and never makes you feel silly for asking.

02 — Personality

Four traits we live by

Every sign, sticker, message, and conversation should carry these four qualities. If something doesn't feel like at least two of them, rework it.

Warm
We greet, we listen, we remember names. The shop feels like walking into a friend's place, not a showroom.
Honest
If a repair costs more than the product is worth, we say so. We quote real prices, real timelines, and no surprise charges.
Skilled
We know our craft. We explain things simply because we understand them deeply — not the other way around.
Dependable
When we say Thursday, we mean Thursday. Predictable, reliable, on time — every single time.
03 — Logo

The mark

The logo is a simple wordmark paired with a circular emblem holding an italic "T". The circle suggests reliability and completeness; the warm italic letter gives it personality and approachability.

T
Tirath
Electronics · Since you needed us

Always

  • Use the full lockup on signage and business cards
  • Keep clear space of at least the height of the "T" emblem around the logo
  • Use on solid backgrounds — cream, forest green, or white
  • Maintain the original proportions when scaling

Never

  • Don't stretch, skew, or rotate the logo
  • Don't change the colors outside the approved palette
  • Don't add drop shadows, outlines, or 3D effects
  • Don't place over busy photos without a solid backdrop

06 — Voice & Tone

How we speak

Warm, plain, and confident. No technical jargon thrown at customers. No "sir/madam" stiffness either — just real, helpful conversation.

Say

"Bring it in — we'll take a look and tell you exactly what's needed. No charge just to check."

Don't say

"Diagnostic charges applicable. Service inspection fee non-refundable."

Say

"Honestly, this one's not worth repairing — but here's a good replacement in your budget."

Don't say

"Repair not feasible. Recommend buying our premium model."

Say

"Ready by Thursday evening. We'll WhatsApp you when it's done."

Don't say

"ETA 48-72 hours subject to parts availability. Customer to follow up."

07 — Applications

Where it lives

The brand is built for a walk-in shop. Everything below should feel like the same place — the same colours, the same voice, the same calm warmth.

i
Shop Signboard
Forest green background, cream Fraunces wordmark. Keep it clean — name, "Electronics", phone number. Nothing else.
ii
Business Cards
Cream paper, forest green ink. Logo on front. Name, role, phone, shop address on back. Matte finish, no gloss.
iii
Repair Receipts
Use Outfit for all line items. Forest green header strip. Include a thank-you line at the bottom in Fraunces italic.
iv
WhatsApp Profile
Square logo emblem (the "T" circle) as profile picture. Bio: "Tirath Electronics · Sales & Repairs · [phone]"
v
Carry Bags & Stickers
Cream bags with forest green logo. Stickers on every repair item — small, clean, with date written by hand.
vi
Posters & Offers
Sun Amber for sale tags and seasonal offers — never for everyday signage. Keep amber rare so it stays meaningful.
